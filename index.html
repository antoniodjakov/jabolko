What we make

Three lines from field to bottle

Each line follows the same practical rhythm: harvest carefully, process cleanly, ferment with patience, and keep batches transparent from start to finish.

Apple cider

Fresh-pressed cider with a clean orchard finish.

Built for crisp drinking, tastings, and seasonal table service. The cider line focuses on bright fruit, balanced acidity, and clean fermentation from press to bottle.

  • Whole-fruit pressing
  • Small fermentation lots
  • Still or lightly sparkling batches

Apple vinegar

Naturally aged vinegar for kitchens and makers.

A vinegar line for salads, marinades, pickling, and fermentation-minded cooks. Patient aging gives the vinegar a rounded apple character with a lively finish.

  • Apple-based fermentation
  • Patient aging
  • Food-friendly acidity

Vine production

Healthy vines managed through the full season.

A clear home for vineyard or vine-growing updates: pruning, trellis work, canopy management, harvest planning, and the move from field work into cellar production.

  • Seasonal pruning
  • Trellis and canopy care
  • Harvest-ready vine management

How it works

A simple production rhythm

Harvest

Pick fruit and grapes at the point where sugar, acidity, aroma, and texture feel balanced.

Press

Prepare clean juice with careful washing, milling, pressing, and first-stage filtering.

Ferment

Track small lots through controlled fermentation, aging, and natural acid development.

Bottle

Package finished batches for tasting, local sales, and future wholesale conversations.
